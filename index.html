The signal

The expertise is real.
The machines
can't read it.

BLR performs strongly in Google AI Overviews, but shows up far less often in ChatGPT. Same attorneys, same library, same 50 years. Google had two decades to learn what blr.com is. LLM crawlers arrived and found a site that tells them nothing about itself.

This is because LLMs don't just "read" your pages; they attempt to map your authority. There is no schema markup anywhere on blr.com. No organization identity on the homepage. No connection between HR Hero and EHS Hero. No distinction between a legal analysis and a product demo. No relationship between an attorney and the content they wrote. The data is in the CMS. None of it is in the markup. BLR answers every question a machine would ask, who you are, what you cover, and who writes it, but only in ways humans can read.

"BLR doesn't have a content problem. It has a translation problem. The authority exists. The machines can't read it yet."

What gets built

From HTML pages
to a knowledge graph.

Right now blr.com is a collection of pages. The fix turns it into a connected graph, where every page carries a declared identity, every content type is classified by intent, and every attorney is linked to the work they authored.

01

Root identity

BLR becomes a named entity on every page. One block. Global. Everything downstream inherits from it.

02

03

Named attorneys connected to their credentials, their firms, and the content they wrote. The verification chain that produces citations.

Gated landing pages structured so AI engines cite BLR without accessing the asset behind the form. The citation is the ad. The gated asset is the product.

AEO Authority Architecture

P0 — Root Identity
BLR exists as a named entity. Crawlers have an unlocked front door.
  • Organization + WebSite schema deployed globally
  • HR Hero and EHS Hero hub schema connected to root
  • BreadcrumbList on all solution pages
  • Sitemap permission errors resolved, Bing submitted
P1 — Content Wiring
648 posts classified. Attorney graph live. Gated pages citable.
  • Each resource type gets its own schema: blogs, guides, news, webinars, case studies, awards, videos, infographics, product demos, product sheets
  • Person schema parsing for all attorney contributors
  • FAQ schema on qualifying gated landing pages
  • Staff editor profiles on /experts/
  • Every schema block tested against Google Rich Results and Schema.org validators. Broken references, missing fields, parse errors caught before they reach a crawler.
