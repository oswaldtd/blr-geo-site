The signal

The expertise is real.
The machines
can't read it.

BLR performs strongly in Google AI Overviews, but shows up far less often in ChatGPT. Same attorneys, same library, same 50 years. Google had two decades to learn what blr.com is. LLM crawlers arrived and found a site that tells them nothing about itself.

This is because LLMs don't just "read" your pages; they attempt to map your authority. There is no schema markup anywhere on blr.com. No organization identity on the homepage. No connection between HR Hero and EHS Hero. No distinction between a legal analysis and a product demo. No relationship between an attorney and the content they wrote. The data is in the CMS. None of it is in the markup. BLR answers every question a machine would ask, who you are, what you cover, and who writes it, but only in ways humans can read.

"BLR doesn't have a content problem. It has a translation problem. The authority exists. The machines can't read it yet."

What gets built

From HTML pages
to a knowledge graph.

Right now blr.com is a collection of pages. The fix turns it into a connected graph, where every page carries a declared identity, every content type is classified by intent, and every attorney is linked to the work they authored.

01

Root identity

BLR becomes a named entity on every page. Global Organization and WebSite schema, plus llms.txt and Bing IndexNow for direct LLM crawler signaling. Everything downstream inherits from it.

02

648 resources classified into three authority tiers. Attorney-tagged content enriched with abstract, about, and mentions fields — the substantive regulatory claims LLMs extract and cite verbatim.

03

Attorney graph

65 named attorneys connected to credentials, firm affiliations, and the content they authored. The verification chain that produces "according to [attorney name]" citations instead of anonymous-page references.

04

Commercial visibility

49 webinars unlocked as citable assets via transcripts. Full marketing-grade schema on HR Hero and EHS Hero product pages — catches "best HR compliance software" queries. The pipeline-relevant layer.

AEO Authority Architecture

P0 — Root Identity · Weeks 1–4
BLR exists as a named entity. Crawlers have an unlocked front door.
  • Organization + WebSite schema deployed globally
  • HR Hero and EHS Hero hub schema connected to root, BreadcrumbList on all solution pages
  • Sitemap resolved and submitted to Bing Webmaster Tools
  • llms.txt deployed and Bing IndexNow API integrated for push-based re-indexation
  • Pre-deployment baseline locked, competitive benchmark captured vs. SHRM, HR Dive, Ogletree Deakins
P1 — Content Wiring · Weeks 5–10
648 posts classified. Attorney graph live. Webinars citable. Product pages discoverable.
  • Conditional schema switch across 648 resource posts, 10 content types, three authority tiers
  • Attorney Person graph: credentials, firm affiliation, sameAs verification for 65 named attorneys
  • Rich Article enrichment — abstract, about, mentions, articleBody for ~188 attorney-tagged posts
  • VideoObject schema with transcripts for 49 webinars — unlocks an entire content class as citable
  • Full marketing-grade SoftwareApplication schema on HR Hero and EHS Hero product pages
  • Staff editor profile inventory on /experts/ + Branch B Person schema
  • Every block validated against Google Rich Results and Schema.org — zero errors, zero warnings
P2 — Measurement · Weeks 11–12
Measured lift. Named-attorney citations. Final report.
  • Five-metric read vs. baseline: velocity, connectivity, attribution, citation diversity, competitive share
  • Named-attorney citation showcase — ChatGPT, Perplexity, Bing examples
  • GA4 segment: LLM-referred traffic, demo form starts, HR Hero and EHS Hero trial starts
  • Final engagement report for BLR leadership — what shipped, what moved, what's next
