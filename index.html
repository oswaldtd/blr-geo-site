The situation

One proof point.
A system worth
building on.

BLR's LLM keyword work produced a 44% month-over-month traffic lift on Handbook Builder. That result matters not as proof that any specific tactic works, but as evidence that AI visibility is a real and movable number for BLR. The question is how to build something repeatable around it rather than treating it as a one-off win.

Right now the GEO strategy exists as two workstreams, HR and EHS, each with a list of planned improvements. Schema, Bing sitemap submission, page structure work. None of it has started. The agency partner is building pages. The window to get ahead of this before competitors move is now, not next quarter.

"The fastest teams don't just publish more. They build systems that detect decay and respond automatically."

That distinction, between shipping tactics and building a system, is what this engagement is about. Schema is the foundation. Measurement is what tells you whether it is working. AirOps is what keeps the system running after the foundation is in place.

What exists
A GEO strategy with HR and EHS workstreams. Profound tracking AI visibility by topic. One confirmed traffic result from LLM keyword work. A plan to add schema, submit to Bing, and audit page structure.
What is missing
A connected framework tying HR GEO and EHS GEO together. Schema implementation across either product. A measurement loop that feeds back into decisions. A decay detection system that catches visibility loss before it compounds.
The opportunity
BLR's compliance content, 50 years of attorney-verified HR and EHS material, is precisely what AI engines want to surface when professionals ask questions. The question is whether BLR is the source that gets cited, or a competitor who built the system first.

A tension worth naming

Gated content is
a business decision.

BLR's guides and whitepapers sit behind forms. That is a lead capture decision, not a GEO mistake. The tension is real though: AI engines cannot read behind a form, which means some of BLR's highest-value thought leadership is not in the retrieval pool.

This engagement does not prescribe what BLR should do with their gated content model. That is a business decision with revenue implications that only BLR can weigh. What it does is make sure the landing pages for gated content are doing as much work as possible, that schema is applied correctly to what is indexable, and that the content structure above the gate is strong enough to surface in AI answers even when the full asset is not accessible.

The question of whether to expose any gated content for AI visibility is worth having. It belongs in the strategy conversation in Month 1, not as a prerequisite to starting.

The approach

Foundation first.
System built on top.

Schema is the foundation. It is the machine-readable layer that tells AI engines what BLR's content is, who wrote it, and how it relates to the entities BLR owns. Without it, AI engines are guessing. Content structure and decay detection are what turn that foundation into a compounding system. The sequence matters because you cannot measure what is working until the foundation is in place.

01
Schema foundation: sitewide, template-level, Week 1

Organization, WebSite, Author, and Person schema go live in the first two weeks. Built at the WordPress template level so every page of a given type is covered automatically, with no per-page work. HR Hero and EHS Hero solution pages follow in Week 3. By end of Month 1 schema runs across the entire site.

The stack

Built for speed
and measurement.

Three tools, used together, are what allow this work to move fast and stay accountable to data rather than assumptions.

Profound

AI visibility tracking by topic. Already in use at BLR. Becomes the measurement layer that drives monthly decisions, not a dashboard to check occasionally.

AirOps

Connects content workflows to AI output at scale. Used to operationalize content structure changes and iterate on what's working without bottlenecking on manual effort.

Schema injected at the template level via wp_head. One implementation covers every page of a given type. No per-page work, no GTM dependency, no render lag.
